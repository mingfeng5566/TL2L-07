Why distance and different materials affect Bluetooth speed?

Path loss refers to the reduction in signal strength of radio waves as they propagate through the air. Path loss, or path attenuation, naturally occurs with distance and is influenced by the signal transmission environment. Obstacles between the transmitter and receiver can disrupt the signal. Attenuators can be anything from humidity and precipitation, to walls, windows, and other obstacles made of glass, wood, metal, or concrete, including metal towers or panels that reflect and scatter radio waves. While radio waves can pass through objects, the amount of attenuation and effective path loss varies with the type and density of the obstacle.

  • Signal Strength (Transmit Power)

  • Interference and Obstacles
  • Environmental Conditions
  • Device Orientation and Position
  • Receiver Quality
  • Power-Saving Modes
  • Frequency Hopping
